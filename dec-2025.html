EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Membership

161 total members as of end of December 2025 (123 active, 38 suspended, 76.4% active rate). January 2026 has already seen active members recover to October levels of 138, confirming the effectiveness of the restructured FOH procedures.

Revenue

Q4 2025 generated $110,067 in gross revenue, with membership revenue representing 84% of total ($92,423). Monthly revenue ranged from $33,805 to $39,829, with November's peak driven by Black Friday promotional activity (package sales up 104% to $6,515).

Utilisation

45.55% in November, declining to 40.98% in December due to seasonal holiday disruption. Current utilisation remains well below the optimal 70-75% target range, indicating significant unused capacity for growth.

Sales vs Cancellations

December saw 7 membership sales vs 14 cancellations. Attrition remained exceptional at 5.70-5.92% through October-November before increasing to 8.38% in December due to seasonal factors—all within acceptable ranges.

Key Issues

  • Utilisation below target: Current 40-45% utilisation remains well below optimal 70-75% target range, indicating significant unused capacity that requires improved member engagement and booking behaviour.
  • Acquisition constraint: Strong retention performance (sub-7% attrition through most of Q4) combined with declining membership sales from 20 to 7 units confirms acquisition rather than retention is the primary growth constraint.
  • Seasonal membership decline navigated: December decline from 169 to 161 total members with 38 suspended members created reactivation opportunity, though January recovery to October levels demonstrates operational resilience.
  • Lead generation seasonal volatility: First visits declined 27% from 83 to 61 in December, reflecting seasonal postponement of fitness commitments, requiring systematic top-of-funnel acquisition initiatives.

Active Interventions Being Deployed

  • FOH restructure completion: Restructured FOH role now encompasses both acquisition and retention with updated weekly schedules, completing the full operational loop. January recovery to October levels (138 active members) confirms effectiveness.
  • Starter Pass promotion: $50 for 15 days unlimited (running until mid-February) drives top-of-funnel acquisition with members receiving $50 back towards ongoing memberships after 5 sessions attendance, converting trials into committed members.
  • Survey findings implementation: Commencing weekly walk clubs, additional classes, and more women's-only sessions based on last year's member survey to drive engagement and optimise utilisation toward 70-75% target.
  • STRONG Challenge: Launching in February to drive engagement, excitement, and team building across the member base.
  • Recovr retention software: Active use commenced, automating re-engagement campaigns, identifying at-risk members, and driving consistent booking behaviour toward capacity targets.
  • Suspension reactivation campaigns: "Come back 3 weeks early, get 1 week free" incentives target the 38 suspended members for Q1 conversion.
  • Pipeline sales process training: Systems training rolled out with ongoing refinement for FOH and trainer acquisition-retention loops to drive membership sales and reduce attrition.

Expected Outcomes

  • Optimise utilisation from current 40-45% toward 70-75% target through survey implementation initiatives (walk clubs, additional classes, women's-only sessions), STRONG Challenge engagement, and Recovr automated re-engagement.
  • Reduce attrition from December's 8.38% back toward sub-7% target (5.70-5.92% demonstrated in October-November) through retention loop refinement and consistent FOH procedures.
  • Convert 38 suspended members through reactivation campaigns, rebuilding active member base above January's recovered level of 138.
  • Drive Q1 acquisition through Starter Pass promotion and pipeline sales process training, addressing the primary growth constraint identified in Q4 performance.

SUMMARY & PATH FORWARD

STRONG Pilates Bankstown navigated Q4 2025's seasonal challenges while maintaining operational fundamentals. The studio ended December with 161 total members (123 active, 38 suspended), down from November's 169 but reflecting expected holiday-period headwinds rather than operational regression. Retention remained strong with attrition at 5.70-5.92% through October and November before increasing to 8.38% in December—all within acceptable ranges given seasonal context. Revenue totalled $110,067 for the quarter, with membership revenue providing 84% of the baseline. The operational framework implementation in October, noted in the previous November report, successfully reversed the prior six-month decline and established consistent processes now being refined and rolled out to other studios.

January 2026 has already confirmed the effectiveness of the restructured FOH procedures, with active members recovering to October levels of 138. The studio is now executing Q1 initiatives: the Starter Pass promotion (running until mid-February) drives acquisition with trial-to-membership conversion incentives; the STRONG Challenge launches in February to drive engagement, excitement, and team building; survey findings are being implemented with weekly walk clubs, additional classes, and women's-only sessions; and ongoing training continues for both FOH and trainer acquisition-retention loops. With 40-45% utilisation indicating significant capacity, Recovr retention software actively automating re-engagement, and suspension reactivation campaigns targeting 38 suspended members, the studio is positioned to reduce attrition toward the sub-7% target while optimising capacity through Q1 2026. The Bankstown team continues to set organisational standards for process rollout excellence, providing a foundation for sustainable growth as the framework matures.

Key Takeaways

  • Q4 2025 navigated seasonal challenges while maintaining operational fundamentals (161 members, $110,067 revenue, 84% from memberships)
  • January 2026 confirms FOH restructure effectiveness—active members already recovered to October levels (138), validating acquisition and retention loop
  • Q1 initiatives executing: Starter Pass (until mid-Feb), STRONG Challenge (Feb), survey implementation (walk clubs, additional classes, women's-only sessions)
  • Significant capacity at 40-45% utilisation combined with Recovr automation and 38 suspended member reactivation opportunities position studio for Q1 growth
  • Bankstown team continues setting organisational standards for process rollout excellence
