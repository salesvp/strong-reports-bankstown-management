EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Membership

169 total members with 142 active and 27 suspended as of January 2026. Active rate improved to 84.0%, up from 76.4% in December. Net membership growth of +12 members (24 sold vs 12 cancelled).

Revenue

Quarterly gross revenue totalled $110,650 across November-January. January recovered to $37,016 following December's seasonal dip to $33,805. Membership revenue contributed 85.2% ($31,546) of January's total. Packages revenue peaked at $6,515 in November (Black Friday) before returning to baseline. Fees revenue reporting discrepancy under investigation with HQ.

Utilisation

January utilisation reached 46.08%, recovering from December's 40.98% low. Sessions attended increased 16.5% from 1,495 (Dec) to 1,742 (Jan). Current utilisation remains below the 70-75% optimal target, indicating significant capacity headroom.

Sales vs Cancellations

January delivered 24 membership sales against 12 cancellations, resulting in net positive growth. Intro offers sold increased from 23 (Dec) to 44 (Jan). First visits grew 44% to 88, indicating improved lead generation.

Key Issues

  • Attrition above target: Current rate of 7.74% remains above the sub-7% target, requiring continued focus on retention initiatives and member engagement
  • Utilisation gap: At 46.08%, utilisation sits well below the 70-75% optimal range, representing untapped capacity and revenue potential
  • Seasonal revenue volatility: December saw 15% revenue decline to $33,805, highlighting vulnerability to holiday seasonality
  • Packages revenue normalisation: Following Black Friday peak, packages revenue returned to baseline at $2,685 in January, presenting growth opportunity
  • Fees reporting discrepancy: Fees revenue showing $0 across all months requires resolution with HQ to ensure accurate financial tracking

Active Interventions Being Deployed

  • STRONG Starter Pass promotion: Currently driving 62 active prospects in trial phase, creating robust conversion pipeline for February
  • FOH restructure: New structure creating seamless customer journey with ongoing refinement and training to optimise sales pipeline efficiency
  • STRONG Intro Offer transition: Mid-February launch of 5 days for $50 offer, which testing has shown increases conversion rates
  • STRONG Challenge: Launching this month to drive class attendance, member engagement, and retention
  • Schedule optimisation: February implementation of schedule changes based on member survey results to better align capacity with demand
  • Recovr retention software: Supporting systematic retention efforts and pipeline management

Expected Outcomes

  • Convert majority of 62 Starter Pass prospects to memberships before mid-February transition
  • Reduce attrition from 7.74% toward sub-7% target through Challenge engagement and retention initiatives
  • Improve utilisation from 46.08% toward 70-75% optimal range via schedule adjustments and increased member engagement
  • Maintain net positive membership growth trajectory established in January (+12 net)
  • Rebuild packages revenue through renewed focus following Black Friday normalisation
  • Establish consistent FOH processes that support sustainable sales performance

SUMMARY & PATH FORWARD

STRONG Pilates Bankstown demonstrated solid recovery in January 2026 following typical holiday seasonality in December. The studio finished January with 169 total members (142 active), reversing the December decline and achieving net positive membership growth with 24 sales against 12 cancellations. The active member rate improved to 84.0%, while suspended members reduced from 38 to 27 as members returned from holiday breaks. Revenue recovered to $37,016 in January, with membership revenue providing a stable foundation at 85.2% of gross revenue. The STRONG Starter Pass promotion has successfully generated 62 active prospects currently in the trial phase, creating a healthy sales pipeline for conversion.

The path forward centres on capitalising on current momentum while addressing key opportunities. Priority areas include: converting the 62 Starter Pass prospects before transitioning to the new STRONG Intro Offer (5 days for $50) mid-February, which testing has shown improves conversion rates; continuing to refine the FOH restructure and sales pipeline processes through ongoing training; implementing schedule changes based on survey results during February to better align capacity with member demand; and driving engagement through the STRONG Challenge launching this month. Reducing attrition from 7.74% toward the sub-7% target and improving utilisation from 46.08% toward the 70-75% optimal range remain ongoing focus areas that will support sustainable growth.

Key Takeaways

  • January recovery saw membership grow to 169 total (142 active) with net +12 member gain
  • 62 active Starter Pass prospects provide strong conversion pipeline for February
  • FOH restructure and ongoing training supporting improved sales processes
  • February priorities: Starter Pass conversions, STRONG Intro Offer launch, schedule adjustments, and STRONG Challenge engagement
  • Key metrics to monitor: attrition toward sub-7% target, utilisation toward 70-75% optimal range
